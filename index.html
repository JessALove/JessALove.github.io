            <h2>About</h2>

            <div class="tile tile-blue">

                <p>Jess Love is a science essayist, journalist, and storyteller interested in complexity, the human mind, and the limits of expertise.</p>

                

                <p>She holds a PhD in cognitive psychology from The Ohio State University, and her work draws on both academic training and years spent translating research for public audiences.</p>

                

                <p>Her essays have appeared in The American Scholar and have been recognized by The Best American Essays series and Longreads, where her most recent work was selected as an editor's pick. From 2011-2014, she wrote the web column "Psycho Babble" about the science of language for The American Scholar. She previously served as Editor in Chief of Kellogg Insight; currently she is Senior Director of the Ryan Institute on Complexity at Northwestern University, where she works on new ways to understand—and communicate—the tangled systems that shape modern life. Also, God help her, she is writing her first novel.</p>
